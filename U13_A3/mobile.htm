Mobile phones

Mobile phones are essential devices that allow people to communicate, access information, and stay connected anytime and anywhere. They combine multiple functions in one compact device, including calling, texting, internet browsing, photography, navigation, and entertainment. Modern mobile phones feature advanced technology such as high-quality cameras, fast processors, large touchscreens, and secure biometric options like fingerprint or facial recognition. Whether used for work, education, or personal use, mobile phones play a vital role in everyday life by making communication faster, easier, and more convenient.

Mobile phones use very similar hardware to a laptop and a desktop computer but in much more compact form, they use something call System-on-a-Chip (SoC) with its CPU and GPU, RAM, storage, cellular modem, sensors, all of these are working together to enable phone functions. How do they work? The SoC runs the opreating system (like Android or iOS) and apps using RAM for active tasks and storage for files. The modem connects to cellular networks, while Wi-Fi/Bluetooth chips handle other wireless communication.
